\section*{Theoretical leg}
Theorem (Augmented-ELBO Equivalence): L\_CD(x\_t, t) = ELBO\_\{A\_$\theta$\}(x\_t, t) + $\epsilon$(t), where $\epsilon$(t) $\to$ 0 as $\Delta$t $\to$ 0 and $\epsilon$(t) $\le$ C · $\|$$\nabla$²s\_$\theta$(x\_t,t)$\|$\_op · $\Delta$t² at finite NFE. Proof: Tweedie's formula applied to consistency-loss term with teacher-score-conditioned augmentation. Empirical falsification: tightness of $\epsilon$(t) bound at finite NFE measured at multiple t-points.

\section*{Engineering leg}
\textasciitilde{}8 lines of PyTorch replacing CD loss term with augmented-ELBO. Parameterize A\_$\theta$ from teacher (existing teacher-score evaluation in CD training, no overhead), sample augmented data, use ELBO gradient. Rest of training pipeline byte-identical.

\section*{Falsification prediction}
\textbf{What we run.} Train 4-NFE consistency-distilled student on CIFAR-10 with EDM teacher (50 NFE), comparing three setups: (a) vanilla CD, (b) SCott stochastic CD, (c) augmented-ELBO loss. Measure FID@4NFE on dev set and verify the equivalence theorem empirically by computing L\_CD vs L\_augmented\_ELBO on held-out batches at t$\in$\{0.005, 0.05, 0.5\}.

\textbf{Success.} FID@4NFE for augmented-ELBO student within 0.3 FID of EDM teacher@50NFE on CIFAR-10 dev; equivalence theorem's $\epsilon$(t) bound holds within numerical precision ($\|$L\_CD - L\_augmented\_ELBO$\|$ < theoretical $\epsilon$ at all 3 t-points) for the augmented-ELBO student.

\textbf{Failure (a) — numerical.} FID@4NFE for augmented-ELBO student does not improve over vanilla CD or SCott (gap unchanged or worse).

\textbf{Failure (b) — mechanistic.} FID improves but the equivalence theorem's empirical $\epsilon$ at boundary t (t=0.005) exceeds the theoretical bound --- indicating the augmented-ELBO interpretation is wrong (the reformulation isn't actually the loss being optimized; the FID gain came from regularization side-effect).

\textbf{Compute budget.} 0.6 GPU-day for the minimal experiment (CIFAR-10, single seed, 3 setups $\times$ 4 t-points probed). User has 4$\times$A100$\times$3wk $\approx$ 84 A100-day; budget is 2 orders of magnitude under available --- feasible.

\textbf{Decision rule.} if failure\_a or failure\_b $\to$ kill

\section*{Mechanism probe}
Compute L\_CD(x\_t, t) and L\_augmented\_ELBO(x\_t, t) on the same held-out batches at t$\in$\{0.005, 0.05, 0.5\} --- the equivalence theorem says they should agree within $\epsilon$(t). Discriminating outcome: empirical |L\_CD - L\_augmented\_ELBO| $\le$ $\epsilon$\_theoretical at each t for the augmented-ELBO student; for vanilla CD student the gap is unbounded at t=0.005. This distinguishes 'reformulation IS the loss' (success) from 'reformulation is just a regularizer' (failure\_b).

\section*{Differentiation from recent literature}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{dblp:conf/aaai/LiuXYDCTFLZ25}: SCott introduces stochasticity into CD as a heuristic relaxation; we derive the relaxation as a specific A\_$\theta$ that satisfies the ELBO equivalence --- SCott's stochasticity recovered as a special case of A\_$\theta$'s noise schedule, not introduced as independent design choice.
  \item \textbf{dblp:conf/nips/0001LYLWLD0W24}: Motion CM disentangles motion vs appearance trajectories empirically; our derivation shows disentanglement is sufficient (lower Lipschitz at boundary satisfies our smoothness condition on A\_$\theta$) and need not be domain-specific to video.
  \item \textbf{arxiv:2603.08709v1}: Scale Space Diffusion connects diffusion timesteps to scale-space conceptually; we produce a closed-form algebraic identity with executable training change, not just conceptual reframing.
  \item \textbf{arxiv:2604.10857v1}: Query Lower Bounds gives information-theoretic minimum on score evaluations; our equivalence theorem characterizes when the consistency-loss objective at finite NFE is well-specified --- lower bound says 'few queries achievable in principle', our theorem says 'how to specify the loss to actually run there'.
\end{itemize}
